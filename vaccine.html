Side Effects

1. Injection Site Reactions: It is common to experience pain, redness, or swelling at the injection site. This reaction usually subsides within a few days and is considered a normal response to the vaccine.

2. Fatigue: Some individuals have reported feeling tired or experiencing fatigue after receiving the Pfizer vaccine. This is typically a temporary side effect and should resolve within a few days.

3. Muscle Pain: Muscle pain or myalgia has been observed in some individuals who received the Pfizer vaccine. This discomfort is generally mild and tends to resolve within a few days.

1. Lymphadenopathy: Lymphadenopathy is the medical term for lymph node swelling. Some people who received the Moderna vaccination have complained of transient lymph node swelling, especially in the arm where the vaccine was given. Usually, this adverse effect goes away on its own in a few days.

2. Vomiting and Nausea: Although it is rare, some people have mentioned feeling sick after receiving the Moderna vaccine. Typically, these symptoms are minor and go away on their own.

3. Allergic Reactions: Although they are uncommon, allergic reactions to vaccines, including the Moderna vaccine, are possible. Hives, itchiness, breathing issues, and swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat are possible side effects for some people.

1. Blood Clots: In rare cases, individuals who received the Johnson & Johnson vaccine have developed blood clots, particularly in combination with low levels of platelets. These blood clotting events are extremely rare but have been observed primarily in women under the age of 50.

2. Fatigue and Body Aches: Some individuals have reported experiencing fatigue and body aches after receiving the Johnson & Johnson vaccine. These symptoms are typically mild and temporary, resolving within a few days.

3. Injection Site Reactions: Similar to other vaccines, pain, redness, or swelling at the injection site can occur with the Johnson & Johnson vaccine. These local reactions are usually mild and resolve on their own within a few days

1. Injection Site Reactions: It is common to experience pain, redness, or swelling at the injection site with Covishield. This reaction is typically mild and temporary, resolving within a few days.

2. Flu-like Symptoms: Some individuals may experience flu-like symptoms after receiving the Covishield vaccine. These symptoms can include fever, headache, fatigue, muscle or joint pain, and general malaise. Most people find these symptoms to be mild and self-limiting.

3. Allergic Reactions: While rare, allergic reactions to the Covishield vaccine can occur. Symptoms may include hives, itching, swelling of the face or throat, difficulty breathing, or a rapid heartbeat. If any signs of an allergic reaction are noticed, immediate medical attention should be sought.

1. Injection Site Reactions: It is common to experience pain, redness, or swelling at the injection site with Covishield. This reaction is typically mild and temporary, resolving within a few days.

2. Fatigue and Malaise: Some individuals have reported experiencing fatigue, tiredness, or a general feeling of malaise after receiving the Covaxin vaccine. These symptoms are usually mild and transient, improving within a short period.

3. Headache and Body Aches: Headaches and body aches have been reported as potential side effects of Covaxin. These symptoms are typically mild and short-lived, resolving on their own without complications.

1. Thrombosis with Thrombocytopenia Syndrome (TTS): A rare but serious side effect that has been associated with the AstraZeneca vaccine is thrombosis with thrombocytopenia syndrome (TTS). It involves the formation of blood clots in combination with low platelet levels.

2. Gastrointestinal Symptoms: Some individuals who received the AstraZeneca vaccine have reported experiencing gastrointestinal symptoms. These can include nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, or abdominal pain.

3. Flu-like Symptoms: As with many vaccines, flu-like symptoms may occur after receiving the AstraZeneca vaccine. These symptoms can include fever, fatigue, muscle aches, headache, and general malaise.

1. Elevated Heart Rate: Some individuals who have received the Sputnik V vaccine have reported experiencing an elevated heart rate or palpitations. While this side effect is rare, it should be monitored and reported to healthcare professionals if it persists or becomes severe.

2. Allergic Skin Reactions: Allergic skin reactions, such as rashes or itching, have been reported as potential side effects of the Sputnik V vaccine. These reactions are typically mild and localized, resolving without complications in most cases.

3. Blood Clotting Disorders: In rare instances, the Sputnik V vaccine has been associated with blood clotting disorders, including deep vein thrombosis (DVT) or thrombocytopenia.
